Sothern and Northern Hemisphere -
WInd Speed(mph) vs. Latitude Linear Regression
  • The line of regression shows that the Latitude and Wind Speed have a VERY WEAK correlation.
  • There are near equal number of cities with varied percentage of wind speeds across all latitudes in SH. The maximum wind speed for most of the cities is between 0 mph and 10 mph. There were some cities with windspeeds between 10 mph and 15 mph. A small percentage of cities across all latitudes were present with wind speeds more than 15 mph.
  • There are near equal number of NH cities with varied percentage of wind speeds across all latitudes. The maximum wind speed for most of the cities is between 0 mph and 10 mph. There were some cities with windspeeds between 10 mph and 20 mph. A small percentage of cities across all latitudes were present with wind speeds more than 20 mph.

Analysis and Observations: Latitude does not have much affect on wind speeds either.

The maximum wind speed for most of the cities was between 0 mph and 10 mph. There were some cities with windspeeds between 10 mph and 20 mph. A small percentage of cities across all latitudes were present with wind speeds more than 20 mph. Why are there fewer windy cities past the latitudes of 60 N and 50 S? There are simply fewer cities near the Antarctic and Arctic Cirles.
